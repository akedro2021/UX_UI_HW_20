Wolf Creek Habitat & Rescue Responsive Web Redesign

A new approach to the web presence of this nonprofit gives the organization a clear brand image & visual consistency across its pages. Design clarity was utilized to increase the awareness of rules for in-person visits to the habitat.

Current Site

New Desktop Website

Overview

Role

UX Designer within a team of 4

Timeline

3 weeks

Tools

Figma, Google Suite, Adobe Suite, Miro, Canva

Deliverables

Web Design viewable in mobile & desktop viewports & a case study presentation

Context

Wolf Creek has a lean business model as a non-profit functioning entirely on camping & visitor fees, donations, one sponsorship program and volunteerwork. The organization benefits from online traffic to bring people for in-person experiences as they do not have an online POS system.

Problem

The owner & users confirm that the rules of interaction & visitation are not easily found & scanned within the website appearance & flow. The web presence needs to be made more accessible in more viewports and with more legible aesthetics.

Objective

Update the website to make finding the rules for interaction & visitation very clear for users and arrange & style the content of the website to make viewing more delightful & legible.

Solution

The new website was designed and iterated with user feedback to ensure that it communicates the prioritized information in a clear manner, so users are prepared for a visit!

The Design Process

Research Phase

User Interviews

In user interviews, our team inquired about how people feel about animal sanctuaries & rescues. Furthermore, investigating user motives for coming to a website for such non-profit organizations gave us general & specific information about the target group of users.

Some themes among users with respect to wildlife habitats/sanctuaries & their websites.

  • Users visit the sites for general info
    • Location
    • Hours of Operation
    • Animal Content
    • Mission Statement
  • Users are open to in-person interactions with animals, but want more information about ethics & safety beforehand.
  • Users believe wildlife education is important, but opinions on zoos vs. independent nonprofit biodiversity organizations are mixed.

User Interview Data

Usability Tests

In usability testing, users were asked to complete a few basic tasks in line with what user interviews reported were important in websites for similar organizations.

  • Task #1: Users were asked to locate a mission statement for the organization.
    • None of the users reported that they could find a mission statement
    • Some users reported that they were skeptical of the authenticity of the website based on the donate button's appearance and percieved functionality.
  • Task #2: Users were asked to find and discern the rules for visitation & interaction at the organization.
    • Just over half of the users reported they could find the information about visitation & interaction.
    • A significant majority of users reported that the rules for interaction were too cluttered to truly discern anything easily.
    • Users generally blamed the wide variety of stylings that the rules of interaction has. "Everything looks important, so nothing is important"

Define Phase

User Persona

We synthesized our user interview data into a user persona. Laura is a hard working mother who likes to plan adventurous outings with her family in the summer. She has teenage children and she wants to be rather quick but thorough with her planning because she has strong values to which to adhere, but doesn't have a lot of free time to sift through online content for potential activities she may be interested in.
